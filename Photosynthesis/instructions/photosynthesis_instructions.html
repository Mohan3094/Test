Intructions

  1. Using a hole punch, punch holes on the leaf surface (between the major veins). Prepare about 40 - 50 leaf disks.
  2. Make sure that the leaf disks are of uniform size and are not damaged.
  3. Use a syringe without a needle. Place 10 of these leaf disks inside a syringe.
  4. Now gradually push the plunger of the syringe inward.
  5. Gently draw a small amount of the CO2 solution (from beaker A) through the syringe tip.
  6. Tap the syringe to dislodge disks that are stuck to the sides.
  7. Hold the syringe vertically, with the tip pointed upwards.
  8. Push in the plunger to expel the trapped air.
  9. Close the tip of the syringe with your finger and pull on the plunger to create a vacuum for 10 - 15 seconds. This will expel the gas from the leaf. You will notice that the leaf disks begin to sink in the syringe.
  10. Gradually release the plunger of the syringe. This will enable entry of the CO2 liquid into the leaf disk.
  11. Repeat this cycle (step 7, 8, 9, and 10) for 3 times. This increases the density of the disks causing them to sink.
  12. Hold the syringe above beaker A.
  13. Pull the plunger out to decant the syringe contents into the beaker. You will notice that the leaf disks will sink to the bottom of the beaker.
  14. Place the beaker slightly away from the light source.
  15. Turn on the light source and start the timer. Observe the setup for 5 minutes. Note down the number of disks that float during this time period.
